Biography

Canadian pop singer Justin Bieber was barely in his teens when he released his 2009 debut, My World, and it became one of the youngest success stories of contemporary pop when it became platinum or double platinum in several countries. Over the next few years, the successive releases, which include compilations of remixes, acoustic sets, a documentary link, a Christmas album and even some appropriate studio recordings, shot up at a dizzying pace. Bieber developed a massive global follow-up, called Beliebers. At the beginning of the decade of 2010, its recording activity slowed down, but it recovered in the middle of that decade with the album Purpose nominated to the Grammy 2015.

Bieber, originally from Stratford, Ontario, took second place in a local singing competition in 2007 and began publishing his performances on YouTube. The videos caught the attention of Scooter Braun, a talent agent and former marketing executive of So So Def, who helped Bieber get an impromptu audition with R & B star Usher. Impressed by what he saw, Usher, along with the president of Island / Def Jam L.A. Reid quickly signed Bieber, 15, for a recording contract.

Bieber released his first single, "One Time," in May 2009. Supported by a popular video that featured an appearance by Usher, "One Time" went platinum in both Canada and America, a feat that was replicated later that year with the release of My World. The disc was technically an EP, featuring only seven songs, but it reached number six on the Billboard 200 album chart and sold over a million copies. Less than a year after his debut, Bieber returned with the "second half" of My World, to ten-song release titled My World 2.0. Further demonstrating his and his management's marketing power, over 8,000 fans who pre-ordered My World 2.0 had his shots used in a photo mosaic (formed to look like a portrait of Bieber) that was displayed on the back side of the disc's booklet. The release topped the Billboard 200. A few weeks later, a combination of My World and My World 2.0, called My Worlds, appeared in some territories.

By the end of 2010, Bieber issued My Worlds Acoustic, a set featuring acoustic versions of nine songs off the My World discs, as well as one new song. The following February, the 3-D documentary Never Say Never was released to theaters. The seven-track Never Say Never: The Remixes, released just days after the film, maintained the flow of Bieber product, and featured appearances from the likes of Kanye West, Usher, and Miley Cyrus. Just in time for Christmas 2011 came to the holiday-themed Under the Mistletoe, complete with several celebrity guest duets and an original song, "Mistletoe," for the first single, which entered the Holiday Songs chart at number two.
